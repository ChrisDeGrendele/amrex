9 /*
10  EBFluxRegister is used for refluxing, re-redistribution,
11  re-refluxing, and re-re-redistribution. See `Tutorials/EB/CNS` for
12  an example of using this class.
13 
14  Reflux for EB is somewhat similar to non-EB. At the beginning of a
15  coarse step, `reset()` is called. In MFIter for the coarse level
16  advance, `CrseAdd` is called with coarse flux. There are two
17  versions of `CrseAdd`, one for regular fab/tile and the other for
18  fab/tile containing cutcells. For the cutcell version, the flux is
19  is at centroid, not face center. The flux is not scaled. In MFIter
20  for the fine level advance, `FineAdd` is called. There are also two
21  version of `FineAdd`, one for regular and the other for fab/tile
22  containing cutcells. The cutcell version also takes `dm` for
23  re-redistribution explained below. After the fine level finished
24  its time steps, `Reflux` is called to update the coarse cells next
25  to the coarse/fine boundary. Note that re-redistribution is also
26  performed in `Reflux`.
27 
28  Re-redistribution is unfortunately more complicated. The coarse
29  level needs to accumulate the *density* (e.g., g/cm^3 for mass
30  density) *loss* of coarse/fine boundary cells (i.e., coarse cells on
31  the coarse side of the coarse/fine boundary) due to redistribution
32  from/to coarse cells covered by the fine level. If a coarse/fine
33  boundary cell redistributes mass to a covered cell, this counts as a
34  positive loss. Here covered means covered be the fine level, not
35  EB. If a covered cell redistributes mass to a boundary cell, this
36  counts as a negative loss for that boundary cell. The accumulation
37  as the coarse level is performed on `FArrayBox` returned by
38  `EBFluxRegister::getCrseData(MFIter const&)`. EBFluxRegister also
39  has `getCrseFlag(MFIte cons&)` function returning `IArrayBox` that
40  contains flags for genuine coarse cell, coarse/fine boundary cells
41  and covered cells.
42 
43  The fine level in re-redistribution needs to accumulate the *mass*
44  (e.g., g for density) *gain* of ghost cells due to redistribution
45  from/to valid cells. The application code can use a local
46  `FArrayBox` to store this and then EBFLuxRegister::FineAdd is called
47  to add the part in ghost cells (excluding ghost cells covered by
48  valid cells of other grids) to EBFluxRegister's internal data.
49 */
